## Delhi Areas Coverage

### Central Delhi Areas
- **Connaught Place (CP)**: Premium area, higher prices, good for tourists
- **Karol Bagh**: Mix of street food and restaurants, moderate prices
- **Chandni Chowk**: Traditional street food hub, very crowded
- **Paharganj**: Budget-friendly, backpacker area

### South Delhi Areas  
- **Lajpat Nagar**: Famous for momos and chaat, evening crowds
- **Greater Kailash (GK)**: Upscale area, fusion street food
- **Saket**: Mall area, food courts and street vendors
- **Alaknanda**: Local markets, authentic flavors

### North Delhi Areas
- **Pitampura**: Residential area, family-friendly options
- **Rohini**: Suburban, good for families
- **Anand Vihar**: Transport hub, quick bites

### East Delhi Areas
- **Laxmi Nagar**: College crowd, cheap eats
- **Preet Vihar**: Local favorites, evening spots

### West Delhi Areas
- **Rajouri Garden**: Shopping area, diverse food options
- **Janakpuri**: Residential, local street food

## Popular Street Foods by Area

### Connaught Place (CP)
**Best Time**: Evening (6-10 PM)
**Crowd**: High, especially weekends
**Price Range**: ₹80-200 per item

- **Momos**: Janpath area, ₹70-90
  - *Tip*: Avoid stalls right at metro exits, walk a bit inside
- **Chaat**: Palika Bazaar area, ₹60-120
- **Rolls**: Bengali Market, ₹80-150
- **Kulfi**: Connaught Circus, ₹40-80

### Lajpat Nagar
**Best Time**: Evening (5-9 PM)  
**Crowd**: Very high after 6 PM
**Price Range**: ₹40-150 per item

- **Momos**: Central Market, ₹50-80
  - *Famous*: Dolma Aunty momos (if still there)
- **Chhole Bhature**: ₹80-120
- **Gol Gappe**: ₹30-50
- **Fruit Chaat**: ₹60-100

### Chandni Chowk
**Best Time**: Morning (10 AM-1 PM) or Evening (6-9 PM)
**Crowd**: Extremely high, narrow lanes
**Price Range**: ₹20-100 per item

- **Paranthe Wali Gali**: ₹60-120 per parantha
- **Jalebis**: Old Famous Jalebi Wala, ₹40-80
- **Chaat**: Multiple vendors, ₹30-70
- **Lassi**: ₹40-80
- *Warning*: Very crowded, watch your belongings

### Karol Bagh
**Best Time**: Evening (6-10 PM)
**Crowd**: Moderate to high
**Price Range**: ₹50-180 per item

- **Chole Kulche**: ₹70-100
- **Rajma Chawal**: ₹80-120  
- **Momos**: ₹60-90

## Time-Based Recommendations

### Morning (8 AM - 12 PM)
- **Best Areas**: Chandni Chowk, local markets
- **Recommended**: Paranthas, Aloo Puri, Lassi, Jalebis
- **Avoid**: Mall food courts (not open), evening-specific vendors

### Afternoon (12 PM - 5 PM)  
- **Best Areas**: CP, Karol Bagh, residential areas
- **Recommended**: Chaat, Momos, Rolls, Cold drinks
- **Note**: Many street vendors take afternoon break (2-4 PM)

### Evening (5 PM - 10 PM)
- **Best Areas**: All areas active, peak time
- **Recommended**: Everything available, best variety
- **Crowd Warning**: Expect queues at popular spots

### Late Night (10 PM onwards)
- **Best Areas**: CP (limited), Karol Bagh
- **Recommended**: Momos, Rolls, Maggi points
- **Limited Options**: Most street vendors close by 10 PM

## Hygiene & Safety Tips
- **Green Signal**: Busy stalls with high turnover
- **Yellow Signal**: Check oil color, cleanliness
- **Red Signal**: Stagnant water, flies, old oil
- **Best Practice**: Eat where locals eat, avoid isolated vendors

## Seasonal Considerations

### Summer (April-June)
- Prefer: Cold items, lassi, kulfi, fresh juices
- Avoid: Heavy fried items during peak heat
- Timing: Early morning or late evening

### Monsoon (July-September)  
- Prefer: Hot items, avoid street-side vendors
- Caution: Extra hygiene care needed
- Best: Covered market areas

### Winter (October-March)
- Perfect for: All street foods
- Special: Hot jalebis, samosas taste better
- Peak Season: Best time for street food exploration

## Local Slang & Expressions
- "Bhaiya" - Brother (addressing vendor)
- "Thoda teekha" - A bit spicy
- "Kam masala" - Less spice
- "Kitne ka?" - How much?
- "Saaf hai?" - Is it clean/fresh?
- "Zyada bheed hai" - Too crowded
